# Normalization Rules

These rules merge selected historical predecessor/successor identities for the historical football
strength index. The goal is continuity for World Cup football records, not geopolitical commentary.

The raw CSVs remain unchanged in `data/worldcup/raw/`. Normalization is applied only in processed
feature tables generated by `npm run build:features`.

## Applied Rules

### Germany

- Original identities: `Germany` (`T-31`, `DEU`) and `West Germany` (`T-86`, `DEU`)
- Normalized identity: `Germany` (`N-DEU`, `DEU`)
- Rationale: FIFA and common football-history summaries generally treat West Germany's World Cup
  record as part of Germany's national-team record.
- Impact on feature calculations: appearances, titles, finals, semi-finals, goals, wins, losses
  conceded, and average finish are aggregated. Germany moves from a separate post-1994 row to the
  combined historical record.

### Russia

- Original identities: `Russia` (`T-62`, `RUS`) and `Soviet Union` (`T-72`, `SUN`)
- Normalized identity: `Russia` (`N-RUS`, `RUS`)
- Rationale: Russia is commonly treated as the football successor for Soviet Union records in many
  historical summaries, while keeping other former Soviet republics separate.
- Impact on feature calculations: Soviet Union tournament history is aggregated into Russia's
  historical index row. This increases Russia's appearances and knockout-stage record.

### Serbia

- Original identities: `Serbia` (`T-66`, `SRB`), `Serbia and Montenegro` (`T-67`, `SCG`), and
  `Yugoslavia` (`T-87`, `YUG`)
- Normalized identity: `Serbia` (`N-SRB`, `SRB`)
- Rationale: Serbia is commonly treated as the football successor for Yugoslavia/Serbia and
  Montenegro continuity in international football records.
- Impact on feature calculations: appearances, match results, goals, and tournament-stage results are
  aggregated into Serbia's historical index row. Croatia, Slovenia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and other
  successor national teams remain separate because they have distinct modern football records.

### DR Congo

- Original identities: `Zaire` (`T-88`, `COD`)
- Normalized identity: `DR Congo` (`N-COD`, `COD`)
- Rationale: Zaire is the former country name for the Democratic Republic of the Congo and uses the
  same FIFA country code lineage in this dataset.
- Impact on feature calculations: the 1974 Zaire appearance is represented as DR Congo in normalized
  processed tables.

## Explicit Non-Merges

### East Germany

- Original identity: `East Germany` (`T-24`, `DDR`)
- Normalized identity: unchanged
- Rationale: East Germany fielded a separate national team and is not merged into Germany for this
  first conservative normalization pass.
- Impact on feature calculations: East Germany's 1974 appearance remains separate.

### Czechoslovakia, Czech Republic, and Slovakia

- Original identities: `Czechoslovakia` (`T-21`, `CSK`), `Czech Republic` (`T-20`, `CZE`), and
  `Slovakia` (`T-68`, `SVK`)
- Normalized identity: unchanged
- Rationale: Czech Republic and Slovakia are both successor national teams, so assigning the full
  Czechoslovakia record to one would be a stronger assumption than this project should make.
- Impact on feature calculations: Czechoslovakia remains a separate historical row.

### Korea, China, and Ireland Variants

- Original identities include separate rows such as `South Korea`, `North Korea`, `China`, `Chinese
  Taipei`, `Republic of Ireland`, and other distinct football associations.
- Normalized identity: unchanged
- Rationale: These are distinct football associations in the dataset.
- Impact on feature calculations: each remains separate.

## Scoring Impact

Normalization changes all aggregate features for merged identities:

- `world_cup_appearances`
- `titles`
- `finals_appearances`
- `semi_final_appearances`
- `total_matches_played`
- `total_wins`
- `goals_scored`
- `goals_conceded`
- `win_rate`
- `average_finish_position_estimate`
- `historical_strength_score`

The comparison output is generated at:

`data/worldcup/processed/normalization_comparison.csv`
